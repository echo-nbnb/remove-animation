# Product

## Register

product

## Users

This is a local installation demo surface used by the project maker during rehearsals and iteration. The primary job is to preview a seven-act furniture scene, tune camera values, and verify particle, material, character, and transparent-video timing before connecting Arduino controls.

## Product Purpose

The page renders a Three.js living-room scene from GLB furniture assets. It supports act switching, particle assembly, material reveal, static floor and door blocks, transparent MOV overlays, and visible camera/scene values for later adjustment.

## Brand Personality

Quiet, theatrical, precise. The interface should stay out of the way so the background images, furniture particles, and story timing remain the main experience.

## Anti-references

Avoid decorative dashboards, heavy panels, oversized marketing sections, and UI that covers the top or side of the scene. Avoid arbitrary render priorities that make depth feel fake.

## Design Principles

Keep the scene first.
Make timing observable and adjustable.
Use restrained controls at the bottom only.
Preserve the source model placement and scale unless an act specifically needs staging changes.
Prefer normal 3D depth behavior over manual layer tricks.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use keyboard act switching for rehearsal. Keep text contrast readable over light backgrounds. Respect reduced-motion preferences for UI transitions, while the 3D scene itself remains a directed demo effect.
